PROJECT REPORT

INTRODUCTION TO THE REPORT

This serves as an introductory section explaining the purpose of the reports and how they are structured. It can outline the following:
Project Background : A brief explanation of the motivation and objectives behind the project.
Report Purpose : Why these reports were created and how they are meant to benefit readers (e.g., farmers, students, researchers, policymakers).
Report Structure : An overview of how the reports are organized (weekly reports, final report, and data analysis). . .

WEEKLY REPORT

Since the project spanned over six weeks, a breakdown of weekly activities and progress helps provide a clear timeline of how the project evolved. Each week’s report can contain:

WEEK-1 Planning and Initial Research


Objective : Explain the initial goals and planning for the project, including forming the team and deciding on key deliverables.
Activities : Summarize how the team began researching plant diseases (e.g., identifying common diseases, gathering background information).
Outcomes : Early findings or ideas generated, and any obstacles faced in gathering initial data.

WEEK-2 Field Visits and Surveys


Objective: Detail the visits made to local farms or gardens to observe plant diseases firsthand.
Activities: Include interactions with farmers, observations of diseased plants, and the distribution of surveys.
Outcomes: A summary of findings from the field, such as the prevalence of specific diseases, challenges faced by farmers, and any adjustments made to the project's approach based on feedback

.

WEEK-3 : Survey Collection and Data Analysis


Objective: Focus on collecting and analyzing survey responses from participants.
Activities: The process of compiling survey results, cleaning data, and using analytical tools (e.g., Excel, Google Forms) to extract meaningful insights.
Outcomes: Key trends observed from the data, such as common diseases affecting crops, farmers’ understanding of plant diseases, or preferred prevention methods.

WEEK-4 : Expert Consultations and Research


Objective: Discuss consultations with agricultural experts, researchers, or extension officers for deeper insights.
Activities: Summarize the interviews, literature reviews, or expert opinions gathered about the latest plant disease research or management strategies. :
