PLANT DISEASES

DEFINITION

A plant disease is a process that occurs over time that alters a plant's normal functions, appearance, or physiological state. It can be caused by a living or non living entity, and can reduce the quality and yield of the plant's harvested product
Plant pathology or phytopathology is the scientific study of plant diseases caused by pathogens (infectious organisms) and environmental conditions (physiological factors).

TYPES OF PLANT DISEASES

Plant diseases are classified primarily by their causative agents, which can be divided into two categories:

BIOTIC OR INFECTIOUS DISEASES:

These diseases are caused by living organisms. They are called plant pathogens when they infect plants. For the purposes of discussing plant pathology, only plant disease pathogens will be discussed. Pathogens can spread from plant to plant and may infect all types of plant tissue including leaves, shoots, stems, crowns, roots, tubers, fruit, seeds and vascular tissues .

ABIOTIC OR NON-INFECTIOUS DISEASES:

These diseases are caused by conditions external to the plant, not living agents. They cannot spread from plant to plant, but are very common and should be considered when assessing the health of any plant. Examples of abiotic diseases include nutritional deficiencies, soil compaction, salt injury, ice, and sun scorch.

CAUSES

There are several types of organisms that cause plant diseases. These include fungi, bacteria, viruses, host:

FUNGI:

Tiny, simple plants called molds, generally measured in microns. No chlorophyll; depends on other organisms or plant tissues for food. Multiply by spores which produce threadlike filaments that enter the host directly or through natural openings.
Advantages: Fungi are important contributors to the soil carbon stock.
Disadvantages: Fungi create harm by spoiling food, destroying timber.

BACTERIA:

Small, one-celled organism, measured in microns .Lack chlorophyll, cannot produce its own .Reproduced by cell division every 15-20 minutes.
Advantages: Protecting them from disease and improving their growth.
Disadvantages: leaf spots and blights, soft rots of fruits, roots, and storage organs, wilts, overgrowths, scabs, and cankers.

VIRUSES:

plant viruses can have a significant negative impact on plants by causing a wide range of symptoms including discolouration, distortion and loss of vigour and yield. .
Advantages: Disadvantages: discolouration, distortion and loss of vigour and yield.

HOST:

A host is a plant that can become infected by a pathogen. Not all plants are susceptible to attack from the same pathogen as some pathogens have preference for certain plants.
Advantages: promoting microbes in defense mechanism against insects and herbivores.
Disadvantages: water scarcity, structural challenges, and the risk of desiccation

HISTORY

Ancient Practices:

Humans have known that plants can become diseased, with subsequent losses in food quantity or quality, for centuries. Early references to plant diseases can be found in the Old Testament of the bible, in the book of Kings, for example, and early Indian, Sumerian and Roman references to plant disease can be found that date back to 2000 bc.

Current Trends:

Plant diseases pose a serious threat to agricultural production and food security. The annual loss of crop yield due to plant diseases is estimated to be more than 30% globally, worth hundreds of billions of dollars .Diseases not only directly lead to crop reduction but also reduce the quality and value of agricultural products, increase agricultural inputs and labor costs, and affect farmers’ income and livelihood .
